| Don’t use the content from this assessment in your dissertation<br><br>Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Deep Learning (DL) are reshaping how problems are solved across every sector—from healthcare to agriculture, media to business. As AI capabilities continue to evolve, professionals must not only be able to apply algorithms effectively but also understand the technical rationale, implications, and emerging trends shaping the future of the field.<br><br>In this assessment, you will take on the role of an AI practitioner tasked with developing a small-scale DL application to solve a real-world problem using open-source data. Besides, you will submit a reflective report that critically analyses your design choices, compares your approach to traditional machine learning methods, and explores the broader implications and sustainability of your work.<br><br>Therefore, you must do the following two parts for this assessment:<br>Part 1: AI or Deep Learning Application<br>Develop a functional DL solution using Python that addresses a well-defined problem using a suitable open-source dataset (subject to approval). You must identify and select a clearly defined problem, and your solution must address it and demonstrate its real-world relevance.<br>As a result, you must submit a reproducible Jupyter Notebook with clear documentation and comments that contains appropriate methods, e.g., data preprocessing, model selection, training, and evaluation, among others. Also, your notebook must contain a README cell to guide markers on how to run your code.<br>You may incorporate pre-trained architectures as appropriate, but you must demonstrate thoughtful design and configuration of your solution, reflecting your understanding of the tools and algorithms used. Although AI assistance is allowed during development, you must adapt and extend any AI-generated content to reflect your understanding and ownership of the work.<br>Note: The problem scope and DL solution cannot be related to transportation and mobility.<br><br>Part 2: Critical Reflection<br>You will write a structured critical reflection that analyses and contextualises your implementation from Part 1. Your reflection must address the following areas:<br>Explain and compare your chosen solution (for your chosen problem) with a traditional machine learning technique and justify the use of deep learning or AI in your project.<br>Reflect on the key design decisions you made, including (but not limited to) model architecture, parameters, and evaluation strategy. Besides, discuss how you used AI assistants (if applicable) to support your implementation and how you expanded or adapted the generated output.<br>Discuss the potential impact of your solution on individuals, organisations, environment or society based on the relevance to your chosen problem.<br>Discuss future trends and opportunities that can apply to your problem and solution. For example, you can identify one or more emerging trends in AI or deep learning and explore how such trends could enhance or transform your solution if implemented in future iterations.<br><br>References from both academic and commercial sources should be included to support your discussion. Ensure your report is clear, objective, and well-structured, adhering to academic standards.<br><br>Additional document will be accessible from the module's Moodle site, as well as an FAQ.<br><br>You can use AI tools in your workflow. However, your application must clearly reflect your own reasoning and decision-making, critical evaluation, original coding effort, design and implementation decisions. Although AI assistance is allowed during code development, you must adapt and extend any AI-generated content to reflect your understanding and ownership of the work. This aims to bridge applied AI development with responsible practice, ethical reasoning, and awareness of industry and research trends. |
